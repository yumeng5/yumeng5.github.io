## Course Overview
This advanced graduate-level course offers a comprehensive exploration of cutting-edge developments in the field of natural language processing (NLP). With Large Language Models (LLMs) serving as the foundation for state-of-the-art NLP systems, we will cover various topics aiming at gaining a better understanding of LLMs' design, capabilities, limitations, and future prospects. Key areas include model architecture and design, training methodologies (e.g., pretraining, instruction tuning, RLHF), emergent capabilities (e.g., in-context learning, reasoning), efficiency (e.g., parameter-efficient training, sparse methods), parametric knowledge with retrieval-augmented generation, language agents, and security and ethics. This course will be highly research-driven with a substantial focus on presenting and discussing important papers and conducting research projects.
